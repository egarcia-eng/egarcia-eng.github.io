About

How I think
about engineering.

My path here wasn't a straight line. I started at Houston Community College, finished my associate in engineering science, and then transferred into the mechanical engineering program at UT Tyler. Somewhere along the way it clicked that I really enjoy the careful, detailed side of the work.

I like problems where small mistakes actually matter. Whether I'm checking a simulation against hand calculations or building an arm that has to do the same thing every single time, what I care about is making something that works reliably and holds up when it counts.

Down the road, I want to keep taking on harder problems where precision and reliability really matter. For now, that means pushing the engineering side as far as I can take it.

Model, then check

I don't take a simulation at face value. I check every result against theory, equilibrium, and a mesh study before I trust it.

Good with constraints

Seal velocity limits, yield stress, a tight compute budget. I treat the limits as the actual problem to solve instead of fighting them.

In it for the long game

I'm using engineering to build the habits I'll need later, and slowly pointing the same focus toward higher stakes, more precise work.

★ Featured Project

Skateboard Deck FEA
Seven plies, three ways.

A three stage finite element study in ANSYS Workbench, looking at how deck curvature and material choice change the way a real skateboard holds up under a hard landing, with every number checked against classical theory.

Open full paper
Objective

Figure out how two ordinary design choices, board curvature and deck material, change the structural behavior of a full skateboard under a realistic impact, then pick the configuration that actually performs best.

Constraints

A 5,000 N load standing in for a 75 kg rider's two foot drop, fixed supports at all four wheels, ANSYS library materials only so anyone can reproduce it, and a single session of compute on a four core machine.

Methodology

I built the full assembly in SolidWorks and brought it into ANSYS through a STEP export, then gave the seven ply maple deck orthotropic properties with custom coordinate systems for the cross grain layers. After a six point mesh convergence study I swapped the maple for an engineered carbon fiber laminate and compared. Every result was checked against classical beam theory, global equilibrium, and a factor of safety.

Roadblocks

The native SolidWorks assembly and a STEP 214 export both locked the geometry in SpaceClaim. I worked through it and found STEP 203 was the only format I could still edit, which isn't documented anywhere in the release notes. The big stress spikes at the truck bolts turned out to be contact singularities rather than real failures, and the explicit impact run ran into a time step and material conflict, so I was upfront that I didn't get it finished.

V90 Twin Cylinder Pump

A purely kinematic design of a 90 degree twin cylinder pump for 10 L/s at 10 bar. I swept 31 crank radii, 5 drive speeds, and 8 link ratios in MATLAB, which is 1,240 geometries, then filtered down to 291 that met every hard constraint using a weighted scoring function.

✦Result: the top design cut peak piston acceleration by 76% and velocity by 52% against the baseline, and matched my SolidWorks Motion check within 0.2%.

Structural Optimization

3D Truss FEA and Optimization

A multi level 3D truss carrying a 95 kN load out at a cantilever node, run in ANSYS for stress, deflection, buckling, and modal response. The starting design blew past the 185 MPa yield limit with a buckling factor of safety of just 0.066, so I reworked the worst members and added cross bracing.

✦Result: deflection down by the 15% I was targeting, and the first natural frequency up 25.4% by turning bending into axial compression.

Impact and Nonlinear Materials

Bullet Impact on an Aluminum Plate

An ANSYS explicit dynamics study of a lead bullet hitting a 100 by 100 by 2 mm nonlinear aluminum plate at both 10 m/s and 478 m/s. I tracked displacement, reaction force, von Mises stress, and the full energy balance through the impact.

✦Result: at 10 m/s the plate held with no perforation, 3.28 mm of peak displacement, and stress leveling off around 273 MPa as the bullet's kinetic energy turned into strain energy.

Color Sorting Robotic Arm

A robotic arm with four degrees of freedom, run by an Arduino, that finds an object, picks it up, and sorts it by color. A TCS34725 color sensor, an ultrasonic sensor, and a light sensor feed a fixed pick and place routine, with the ultrasonic acting as a safety check for hands in the workspace.

✦Result: it sorts red and green objects on its own and stops if a hand gets in the way, with more sensors and actuators than the assignment asked for.

Mechatronics · Embedded

Multi Sensor Thermometer and Alarm

An Arduino alarm system that reads an ultrasonic sensor, an NTC 10k thermistor, and a tilt switch. A 3 point moving average filter cleans up the noise and contact bounce before any threshold logic runs, and a smart timeout handles dropped ultrasonic echoes.

✦Result: the live readings drive a 16 by 2 LCD, a brightness controlled warning LED, and a row of three red LEDs that escalate, all debounced and predictable.
